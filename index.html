Furstenberg Generalized to
Handle the Twin Prime Conjecture

A Furstenberg-style topological proof that $|\mathrm{Irr}_M(G)|=\infty$, where $G=\{b\otimes b : b\text{ irred. in both }L\text{ and }R\}$ generates $K\subseteq T=L\otimes R$. The Key Lemma shows each $\mathfrak{b}\cdot K$ is closed in $K$ via the initial topology and affine structure of left-multiplication; injectivity of $f\!\restriction_K$ (unique factorization) then lets the $(\Leftarrow)$-direction of the initial topology drive the Furstenberg contradiction. Formalized in Lean 4.

$$\mathfrak{b}\cdot K = f^{-1}\!\bigl((b,b)\cdot f(K)\bigr)\cap K\;\text{ closed in }K\quad\xRightarrow{\text{Furstenberg}}\quad|\mathrm{Irr}_M(G)|=\infty\quad\Rightarrow\quad\text{infinitely many twin primes}$$

Key Theorems

The Key Lemma — $\mathfrak{b}\cdot K$ is Closed

For any $\mathfrak{b}=b\otimes b\in B$ (irred. in both $L$ and $R$), left-multiplication by $\mathfrak{b}$ in $K=\langle B\rangle\subseteq T$ satisfies:

$$\mathfrak{b}\cdot K \;=\; f^{-1}\!\bigl((b,b)\cdot f(K)\bigr)\cap K \quad\text{is closed in }K$$

The equality holds because $f\!\restriction_K$ is injective (unique factorization: $\alpha=6b+1$ and $\beta=1-6b$ are $\pm$primes, so UFD in $\mathbb{Z}$ separates words). Closedness then follows from the $(\Leftarrow)$ direction alone: $(b,b)\cdot f(K)$ is closed in $f(K)$ in the product Furstenberg topology (the affine maps $z\mapsto\alpha z+b$ and $w\mapsto\beta w+b$ have nonzero slopes, so their images land in single residue classes and complements are open), and the initial topology on $T$ pulls closed sets back for free.

Why Surjectivity is Free — and Irrelevant

The topology on $T$ is initial w.r.t. $f$, so closed sets of $K$ are exactly $f^{-1}(C)\cap K$ for $C$ closed in $f(K)$. We only need:

$$(\Leftarrow)\quad C\text{ closed in }f(K)\;\Rightarrow\; f^{-1}(C)\cap K\text{ closed in }K$$

Surjectivity of $f\!\restriction_K$ onto $f(K)$ is a tautology (definition of image). Injectivity is the nontrivial content, supplying the reverse inclusion $f^{-1}((b,b)\cdot f(K))\cap K\subseteq\mathfrak{b}\cdot K$. A continuous bijection need not be a homeomorphism, but we never needed the forward direction — only $(\Leftarrow)$, which is free from the initial topology.

Furstenberg Conclusion — Infinitely Many Twin Primes

$$|\mathrm{Irr}_M(G)|=\infty \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \text{infinitely many twin prime pairs}$$

If $\mathrm{Irr}_M(G)$ were finite $=\{\mathfrak{b}_1,\ldots,\mathfrak{b}_m\}$, the covering $K\setminus\{e\}=\bigcup\mathfrak{b}_i\cdot K$ would be a finite union of closed sets each contained in a clopen envelope (product of arithmetic progressions in $\mathbb{Z}^2$). The Empty-or-Infinite theorem then forces $\{e\}$ to be open — but every basic open set in $K$ is infinite. Contradiction. Since irreducibles biject with twin primes (Thm 4.2), we conclude.

Proof at a Glance

Define $L=(\mathbb{Z},*)$ with $x*y=6xy+x+y$ and $R=(\mathbb{Z},\star)$ with $x\star y=-6xy+x+y$, both with identity $0$. The map $\eta(x)=-x$ is an isomorphism $L\cong R$. The maps $\phi(x)=6x+1:(L,*)\to(\mathbb{Z},\cdot)$ and the analogous map for $R$ show both monoids embed into $(\mathbb{Z},\cdot)$. Key consequence: no zero divisors ($u,v\neq 0\Rightarrow u*v\neq 0$).

Form the monoid tensor product $T=L\otimes R$ and the product monoid $S=\mathbb{Z}^2$ with operation $(x,y)(z,w)=(x*z,\,y\star w)$. The homomorphism $f:T\to S$, $f(x\otimes y)=(x,y)$ induces the initial topology on $T$: $U\subseteq T$ open iff $U=f^{-1}(V)$, $V\subseteq S$ open. Define $B_0=\{b\in\mathbb{Z}:6b+1\text{ and }1-6b\text{ are }\pm\text{prime}\}$, $B=\{b\otimes b:b\in B_0\}$, $K=\langle B\rangle\subseteq T$.

Every element of $K$ is a finite product of generators $b_i\otimes b_i\in B$, so $f$ maps it to $(b_1*\cdots*b_n,\;b_1\star\cdots\star b_n)$. The isomorphism $n\mapsto 6n+1$ transforms $*$-products into ordinary integer products of $\pm$primes. By unique factorization in $\mathbb{Z}$, $f\!\restriction_K$ is injective, giving the key equality $\mathfrak{b}\cdot K=f^{-1}((b,b)\cdot f(K))\cap K$.

Left multiplication by $(b,b)$ in $S$ acts as $z\mapsto(6b+1)z+b$ and $w\mapsto(1-6b)w+b$ — two affine maps with slopes $\alpha=6b+1$ and $\beta=1-6b$, both $\pm$prime. Every image element lies in $b+\alpha\mathbb{Z}$ (resp. $b+\beta\mathbb{Z}$), an arithmetic progression. Points outside the image have explicit open Furstenberg neighborhoods disjoint from it (Case 1: wrong residue class mod $\alpha$; Case 2: right class but preimage misses $f(K)$, handled by closedness of $f(K)$). Hence $(b,b)\cdot f(K)$ is closed in $f(K)$, and the initial topology pulls this back to closedness of $\mathfrak{b}\cdot K$ in $K$.

Apply the abstract Furstenberg recipe to $(K,\cdot)$. (R1) Every $k\in K\setminus\{e\}$ has an irreducible divisor (norm descent). (R2) Each $\mathfrak{b}\cdot K$ is closed inside a clopen envelope — the preimage of $(b+\alpha\mathbb{Z})\times(b+\beta\mathbb{Z})$ in $f(K)$. (R3) Every nonempty basic open set in $K$ is infinite (Empty-or-Infinite). If $|\mathrm{Irr}_M(G)|$ were finite, the finite union of closed sets $\bigcup\mathfrak{b}_i\cdot K$ would force $\{e\}$ to be open — contradiction. Therefore $|\mathrm{Irr}_M(G)|=\infty$, and by Thm 4.2 there are infinitely many twin primes. $\blacksquare$
