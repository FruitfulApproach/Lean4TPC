Coset Topologies on Monoid Tensor Products
& the Twin Prime Conjecture

A Furstenberg-style topological proof that the submonoid $N=\langle\{k\otimes k\}\rangle$ of the monoid tensor product $M=(\mathbb{Z},\circledast)\otimes_{\mathrm{Mon}}(\mathbb{Z},\star)$ has infinitely many irreducibles — each corresponding to a twin prime pair. Proving that $N\cap(a+b\mathbb{Z})\times(c+d\mathbb{Z})$ is either empty or infinite is a key structural lemma. Formalized in Lean 4.

$$\widehat{q}\otimes N = N\cap(\widehat{q}\otimes M)\quad\Rightarrow\quad |\mathrm{Irr}(N)|=\infty\quad\Rightarrow\quad\text{infinitely many twin primes}$$

Key Theorems

The Coset Identity (Theorem 3.2)

For any irreducible $\widehat{q}=(q,q)\in\mathrm{Irr}(N)$:

$$\widehat{q}\otimes N = N\cap(\widehat{q}\otimes M)$$

This identity bridges algebra (the coset $\widehat{q}\otimes N$) and topology (the intersection $N\cap(\widehat{q}\otimes M)$) inside the tensor product $M=(\mathbb{Z},\circledast)\otimes_{\mathrm{Mon}}(\mathbb{Z},\star)$. The bilinearity of $\otimes$ is the engine that powers the entire Furstenberg argument for twin primes.

Empty or Infinite (New Theorem)

For any $a,b,c,d\in\mathbb{Z}$ with $b,d\neq 0$:

$$N\cap\bigl((a+b\mathbb{Z})\times(c+d\mathbb{Z})\bigr)\text{ is either }\emptyset\text{ or infinite}$$

Proof sketch: If $(z_0,w_0)$ is in the intersection, then $(z_0,w_0)\otimes\Delta(nbd)\in N$ stays in the same arithmetic progression box for every $n\in\mathbb{Z}$ (since the correction terms are divisible by $b$ and $d$ respectively), and injectivity of $\phi$ gives distinctness.

Main Theorem (Theorem 5.2 + Corollary 5.3)

$$|\mathrm{Irr}(N)|=\infty \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \text{infinitely many twin prime pairs}$$

Furstenberg argument: the covering $N\setminus\{(0,0)\}=\bigcup\widehat{q}\otimes N$ by clopen cosets, combined with the fact that no singleton is open, forces $|\mathrm{Irr}(N)|=\infty$. Since irreducibles biject with twin primes (Thm 4.2), we're done.

Proof at a Glance

Define $x\circledast y = 6xy+x+y$ and $x\star y = -6xy+x+y$. The maps $\phi(x)=6x+1$ and $\psi(x)=6x-1$ are monoid isomorphisms to $(6\mathbb{Z}+1,\cdot)$ and $(6\mathbb{Z}-1,\bullet)$. Key consequence: no zero divisors ($u,v\neq 0\Rightarrow u\circledast v\neq 0$).

$M = (\mathbb{Z},\circledast)\otimes_{\mathrm{Mon}}(\mathbb{Z},\star)$ is the monoid tensor product. The diagonal generators $G=\{k\otimes k\}$ generate the submonoid $N=\langle G\rangle$. Equip $\mathbb{Z}$ with the Furstenberg topology (basis = arithmetic progressions). Then $(M,\otimes)$ is a topological monoid.

$\widehat{a}\otimes M$ is a product of arithmetic progressions, hence clopen. The key lemma: $\widehat{q}\otimes N = N\cap(\widehat{q}\otimes M)$ for $\widehat{q}\in\mathrm{Irr}(N)$. Proof uses primality of $\phi(q)$ and Euclid's lemma. Therefore $\widehat{q}\otimes N$ is clopen in $N$.

$N\cap(a+b\mathbb{Z})\times(c+d\mathbb{Z})$ is either empty or infinite. Proof: if $(z_0,w_0)$ is in the intersection, multiplying by $\Delta(nbd)$ stays in the box and gives infinitely many distinct elements (injectivity via $\phi$). Consequence: $N$ has no isolated points — it's a perfect set in the subspace topology.

In the tensor product $M=(\mathbb{Z},\circledast)\otimes_{\mathrm{Mon}}(\mathbb{Z},\star)$, an element $k\otimes k\in N$ is irreducible precisely when it cannot be non-trivially factored within $N$. The bilinearity of the tensor product ensures that the coset identity $\widehat{q}\otimes N = N\cap(\widehat{q}\otimes M)$ respects the universal property, and irreducibility in $N$ corresponds exactly to twin primality via $\phi$ and $\psi$.

$(z,w)\in\mathrm{Irr}(N) \iff 6z+1,6z-1$ both $\pm$prime. The norm $|(z,w)|=(6z+1)^2$ gives descent: every element has an irreducible divisor. This yields the covering $N\setminus\{(0,0)\}=\bigcup\widehat{q}\otimes N$.

If $\mathrm{Irr}(N)$ finite → covering = finite union of clopens → $N\setminus\{(0,0)\}$ clopen → $\{(0,0)\}$ open → contradiction (no singleton open). Therefore $|\mathrm{Irr}(N)|=\infty$ → infinitely many twin primes. $\blacksquare$
