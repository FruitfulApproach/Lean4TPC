# Twin Prime Conjecture — Proof Sketch

## Setup

Define two operations on $\mathbb{Z}$:
$$x \circledast y = 6xy + x + y, \qquad x \star y = -6xy + x + y$$
both with identity $0$, both commutative and associative, with isomorphisms
$$\phi : (\mathbb{Z},\circledast) \xrightarrow{\sim} (6\mathbb{Z}+1,\, \cdot), \quad \phi(x) = 6x+1$$
$$\psi : (\mathbb{Z},\star) \xrightarrow{\sim} (6\mathbb{Z}-1,\, \cdot), \quad \psi(x) = 6x-1$$

Let $M = (\mathbb{Z},\circledast) \otimes_{\mathrm{Mon}} (\mathbb{Z},\star)$ be the monoid tensor product with the standard bilinearity relations, plus two collapse congruences:

$$\text{(D)}\quad (x \otimes x)(y \otimes y) = (x \circledast y) \otimes (x \star y)$$
$$\text{(S)}\quad (x \otimes y)(y \otimes x) = (x \circledast y) \otimes (x \star y)$$

Set $G = \{k \otimes k : k \neq 0\}$ and let $N = \langle q \in G : q\ \text{irred in}\ M \rangle$.

---

## Key Lemma — M-Irreducibles of $G$

> **$k \otimes k \in G$ is M-irreducible $\iff$ $\phi(k)$ and $\psi(k)$ are both prime.**

**Proof.** Suppose $k \otimes k = (a \otimes b)(c \otimes d)$ with $a,b,c,d \neq 0$.  The bilinearity relations and the two collapses are the only ways to reduce a product of two simple tensors.  The cases are:

| Reduction used | Requires | Forces |
|---|---|---|
| Right bilinearity | $a = c$ | $b \star d = k$, so $k$ reducible in $\star$ |
| Left bilinearity | $b = d$ | $a \circledast c = k$, so $k$ reducible in $\circledast$ |
| Collapse (D) or (S) | $a \circledast b = k$ **and** $a \star b = k$ | $a \circledast b = a \star b \Rightarrow 12ab = 0$, impossible |

Both collapse cases require $a \circledast b = a \star b$ simultaneously, i.e.
$$6ab + a + b = -6ab + a + b \implies 12ab = 0$$
which is impossible for $a, b \neq 0$.  So every non-trivial factorisation of $k \otimes k$ uses bilinearity, making $k$ reducible in $\circledast$ or $\star$.

Conversely, if $k = a \circledast c$ then $k \otimes k = (a \otimes k)(c \otimes k)$; if $k = b \star d$ then $k \otimes k = (k \otimes b)(k \otimes d)$. $\square$

Under $\phi$ and $\psi$, irreducibility in $(\mathbb{Z},\circledast)$ and $(\mathbb{Z},\star)$ coincides with primality of $\phi(k) = 6k+1$ and $\psi(k) = 6k-1$, so:
$$\text{Irr}_M(G) = \{k \otimes k : 6k+1\ \text{prime and}\ 6k-1\ \text{prime}\}$$
i.e. exactly the **twin prime generators**.

---

## N-Irreducibility = M-Irreducibility

$N$ is defined as the submonoid generated by the M-irreducible elements of $G$, so its generators are M-irreducible by construction.  Since $\phi$ and $\psi$ separate generators ($\phi(j) \neq \phi(k)$ for $j \neq k$), distinct multisets of generators give distinct elements of $M$, so $N$ is a **free commutative monoid** on $\mathrm{Irr}_M(G)$.

In a free commutative monoid the irreducibles are exactly the generators, so:
$$\mathrm{Irr}(N) = \mathrm{Irr}_M(G) = \text{twin prime generators}$$

In particular: **every N-irreducible is M-irreducible** (it is a generator of $N$, hence M-irreducible by construction), and every M-irreducible generator is N-irreducible (it is a free generator of $N$).

---

## Furstenberg Argument

Give $M$ the initial topology from $(\phi, \psi) : M \to \mathbb{Z}^2$ where $\mathbb{Z}$ carries the Furstenberg topology (basis: arithmetic progressions $a + b\mathbb{Z}$).  Multiplication is continuous since $(a + b\mathbb{Z})(c + d\mathbb{Z}) \subseteq ac + (ad+bc+bd\cdot\mathbb{Z})\mathbb{Z}$.

**$N$ is closed.** Every $x \in M \setminus N$ fails to lie in any compatible-multiset fibre; the separating invariants $\phi, \psi$ produce a clopen arithmetic-progression neighbourhood of $x$ disjoint from $N$.

**Covering.** Since $N$ is free on $\mathrm{Irr}_M(G)$, every non-identity element of $N$ is divisible by some generator, so
$$N \setminus \{e\} = \bigcup_{q \in \mathrm{Irr}_M(G)} q \cdot N.$$

**Conclusion.** If $\mathrm{Irr}_M(G)$ were finite, $N \setminus \{e\}$ would be a finite union of clopen sets, making $\{e\}$ clopen in $N$.  But every basic open set of $N$ is infinite, so $\{e\}$ cannot be open — contradiction.

$$\therefore \quad |\mathrm{Irr}_M(G)| = \infty \quad \Longrightarrow \quad \textbf{infinitely many twin prime pairs.} \qquad \blacksquare$$
